The key of david

Texts: Isaiah 22: 20-22, Isaiah 9:6-7, Luke 1:32, Revelation 3:7-8. A key does two things. It opens and closes a door. Jesus is the owner of the keys to all things. Any door that Jesus opens for you, no one can close it. According to Revelation 3:8, it says \"l know thy works; behold, I have set before thee an open door, and no man can shut it: for thou hast a little strength, and hast kept my word, and hast not denied my name\".
Jesus says your strength is little, that is why you are struggling to get the keys to open the doors to enter into your success, achievement, glory, to have your own children, to get a good job etc.
There are many doors that your past generations were not able to open, but because Jesus Christ owns the keys to all doors, if you believe in Him, He will open all those doors to you and you will get in easily.
Jesus is the way, if you hold unto His words, He will make a way for you.
God is more than able to make way where there's no way.
According to Exodus 14: 10-22, the Israelites couldn't pass through the Red Sea because there was no way for them. But when God instructed Moses to stretched forth hot rod, the door of the Sea was opened and the Israelites passed through the dry land safely while the Egyptians perished in the Sea.

How Can You Enter Through The Doors?.


1 You must engage yourself in a high praise
2 You must continue to serve God
3 Let the Holy Spirit lead you always
4 You must continue to give in season and out of season
5 You must walk with the right company (people)
6 You must continue to grow in faith
7 You must continue to grow in power of the Holy Spirit
8. You must engage in the ministry of the angels.
9 You must continue to grow in spiritual understanding.

Conclusion: The day you accepted Jesus Christ as your Lord and Saviour, those beautiful doors would be opened for you. All you need to do is to ask him to open those doors for you.
Further References: 1sr Corinthians 16:9, Matthew 6:13, Psalms 100:4, Psalms 34:10, Psalms 23:1, Luke 6:38, 1st Corinthians 15:33. - Remain richly blessed in the Lord.
